# Reliable Data Sources by Category

A curated list of data sources to prioritize when conducting market research. Organized by type and geography.

## Tier 1: Most Reliable (Primary Sources)

### Company-Level Data
- **Company blogs and press releases** — Direct announcements, product launches, pricing changes
- **SEC filings / MCA filings** — Revenue, growth rates, customer counts for public companies
- **Annual reports** — Detailed financials and strategy
- **Investor presentations** — Often contain market sizing data companies use to pitch
- **Job postings** — Reveal team size, tech stack, strategic direction, new markets

### Government & Institutional
- **RBI reports** — Indian financial and economic data
- **NASSCOM** — Indian tech and IT industry data
- **Ministry of Education (India)** — Edtech and education sector data
- **Census / NSSO data** — Demographics and economic indicators
- **World Bank Open Data** — Cross-country comparisons

## Tier 2: High Quality (Analyst & Research)

### Industry Reports (Often Paywalled — Search for Excerpts)
- **Redseer Consulting** — Excellent India-specific market sizing, especially consumer tech and edtech
- **Inc42** — Indian startup ecosystem data, funding reports
- **YourStory** — Indian startup coverage and market analysis
- **Tracxn** — Startup and funding data
- **PitchBook / Crunchbase** — Global funding and company data
- **Statista** — Market statistics (verify primary source)
- **Grand View Research / Mordor Intelligence** — Market size reports (directionally useful, verify independently)

### Financial Data
- **Tofler / Zauba Corp** — Indian company financials from MCA
- **Screener.in** — Indian listed company data
- **Moneycontrol** — Financial data and analysis

## Tier 3: Useful but Verify (Community & Aggregated)

### Community Intelligence
- **G2 / Capterra** — Software reviews, competitive positioning, pricing
- **Product Hunt** — New product launches, market trends
- **Reddit** — Authentic user opinions, pain points (search specific subreddits)
- **Twitter/X** — Real-time market sentiment, founder insights
- **LinkedIn** — Company headcount trends, hiring patterns

### News & Analysis
- **Economic Times** — Indian business and market coverage
- **Mint / LiveMint** — Indian financial and startup news
- **TechCrunch** — Global tech and startup news
- **The Ken** — Deep-dive Indian business journalism
- **Morning Context** — Indian tech and business analysis

## Tier 4: Directional Only (Use with Caution)

- **Generic market research sites** — Often recycled data, check methodology
- **Quora answers** — May be outdated or anecdotal
- **AI-generated market reports** — Verify all numbers independently
- **Wikipedia** — Good starting point, always check citations

## Search Tips by Source Type

| What You Need | Where to Look | Search Pattern |
|--------------|---------------|----------------|
| Market size | Redseer, Inc42, industry reports | `[market] market size India 2025 report` |
| Competitor revenue | Tofler, Crunchbase, press releases | `[company] revenue ARR funding` |
| Pricing data | G2, competitor websites, review sites | `[company] pricing plans 2025` |
| Customer sentiment | G2 reviews, Reddit, Twitter | `[product] review complaints reddit` |
| Funding data | Crunchbase, Inc42, Tracxn | `[company] funding round 2025` |
| Team size | LinkedIn, job postings | `site:linkedin.com [company] employees` |
| Technology trends | Gartner hype cycle, ThoughtWorks radar | `[technology] adoption enterprise 2025` |

## Important Notes

1. **Always triangulate** — Never rely on a single source for important numbers.
2. **Check the date** — Market data degrades fast. Always note when data was published.
3. **Understand methodology** — A "market size" based on supply-side revenue is very different from one based on consumer spending.
4. **Watch for bias** — Companies selling into a market tend to overstate TAM. Competitors may downplay each other.
5. **India-specific data is sparse** — For many categories, you'll need to derive India numbers from global data using GDP ratios, internet penetration, or purchasing power adjustments. Always flag when you're doing this.
