# Research Prompts Library

Effective search query patterns organized by research mode. Use these as starting points, then adapt based on what you find.

## Market Sizing Queries

### Finding TAM/SAM Numbers
- `[industry] market size 2025 2026`
- `[industry] market report [geography]`
- `[category] TAM global`
- `[industry] revenue forecast CAGR`
- `[industry] India market size crore`

### Bottom-Up Data Points
- `[target customer type] number of companies India`
- `[industry] average deal size pricing`
- `[industry] customer acquisition cost benchmark`
- `[industry] annual spend per company`

### Growth Signals
- `[industry] funding rounds 2025`
- `[industry] trends 2025 2026`
- `[industry] growth drivers`
- `why [industry] is growing India`

## Competitive Landscape Queries

## Tips for Effective Searching

1. **Start with the most specific query** — you can always broaden.
2. **Use year markers** — add "2025" or "2026" to get recent data.
3. **Try multiple phrasings** — "market size" vs "revenue" vs "TAM" vs "industry report".
4. **Fetch full articles** — web search snippets are often too brief. Use web_fetch on promising results.
5. **Look for data in unexpected places** — investor decks, job postings, and conference talks often reveal data that formal reports don't.
6. **Search in local currency** — for India, search in "crore" or "INR" to find India-specific reports.
