## 3. Executive summary
- India is no longer an emerging data-center market in a loose sense; it is now one of the most actively scaling digital-infrastructure markets in Asia.
- The operator landscape is led by a mix of `global institutional operators` and `India-native infrastructure players`.
- The most important operator set today is: `STT GDC India`, `NTT GDC India`, `Yotta`, `Nxtra by Airtel`, `CtrlS`, `Sify`, `Web Werks-Iron Mountain`, and `AdaniConneX`.
- `Mumbai` remains the anchor market, but `Chennai`, `Hyderabad`, `Noida / Delhi NCR`, `Pune`, and `Bengaluru` are strategically important secondary clusters.
- The market is increasingly bifurcated between:
  - large hyperscale capacity builders
  - enterprise/colo specialists
  - edge/distributed capacity providers
- For investors and strategists, the most important question is no longer whether India will add capacity, but which operators can secure power, land, fiber, and anchor hyperscaler demand most efficiently.

## 4. Market snapshot
### 4.1 Direction of travel
Recent market research from CBRE and other advisers indicates:
- operational capacity continues to scale quickly
- hyperscaler-led demand is a major structural driver
- Mumbai remains the largest cluster by installed base
- southern markets such as Chennai and Hyderabad are strengthening due to connectivity, land availability, and enterprise / cloud demand

### 4.2 Why India remains structurally attractive
The market’s attractiveness comes from:
- public cloud adoption
- enterprise digitization
- AI / data-intensive workloads
- regulatory and localization sensitivity for certain sectors
- submarine cable connectivity and metro-cluster economics
- rising demand from domestic digital platforms, BFSI, SaaS, and global cloud providers

## 6. Geography and clustering
### 6.1 Primary clusters
#### Mumbai
Mumbai remains the anchor data-center market because of:
- financial-services density
- submarine cable connectivity
- enterprise demand concentration
- historic operator presence

#### Chennai
Chennai matters because of:
- subsea cable access
- coastal connectivity logic
- growing cloud and enterprise interest

#### Hyderabad
Hyderabad is increasingly important because of:
- technology ecosystem growth
- competitive land dynamics relative to Mumbai
- increasing hyperscale and enterprise relevance

#### Noida / Delhi NCR
NCR is strategically important because of:
- enterprise demand concentration
- northern market access
- public sector and regulated-sector proximity

### 6.2 Secondary but important clusters
- `Pune`
- `Bengaluru`
- `Kolkata`
- selected edge/distributed nodes attached to telecom or cloud-network strategies

## 8. Growth outlook
### 8.1 24-month read
Over the last 24 months, the market has shifted from “India data centers are growing” to “India data centers are becoming core digital infrastructure.”

The strongest observable patterns are:
- larger campus-scale projects
- more explicit AI / high-density positioning
- continued operator expansion beyond a single-city thesis
- stronger institutionalization of the sector through global capital, JVs, and long-term infrastructure strategies

### 8.2 What to watch next
- power availability and grid constraints
- execution speed of large announced campuses
- AI workload density and cooling requirements
- pricing discipline as more capacity enters the market
- whether secondary cities become real scale markets or remain supplemental
