# Data Centers in India

## Executive Summary
India’s data-center market is no longer a generic infrastructure theme; it is a concentrated operator-and-city landscape led by Mumbai, Chennai, Hyderabad, and Noida, with `STT GDC India`, `CtrlS`, `Yotta`, `Sify`, and `Nxtra` as the most visible operators. The main improvement over Batch 001 is that this pass explicitly separates total market metrics from operator metrics and surfaces where numbers are not directly comparable across sources.

## Scope
- **Category**: colocation and hyperscale-oriented data-center infrastructure in India.
- **Decision uses**: investor briefing, landscape benchmarking, partner and customer strategy.
- **Time window**: last 24 months, with current operator pages and recent market updates.

## Market Size and City Structure
- `JLL` says India's live colocation capacity crossed **1,123 MW in H1 2025**, with Mumbai still the largest cluster and Chennai/Hyderabad/Noida scaling fast. Source: [JLL India data centers outlook](https://www.jll.com/en-in/insights/market-dynamics/india-data-centers).
- `CBRE` reported a broader installed base of roughly **1.53 GW by September 2024**, plus large under-construction capacity. Source: [CBRE 2024 India Data Centre Market Update](https://www.cbre.com/insights/reports/2024-india-data-centre-market-update).
- These numbers should not be treated as directly identical because broker methodologies differ on what counts as live IT load, commissioned shell, and hyperscale inventory.

## City-Level Structure
### Mumbai
- Still the primary data-center cluster because of subsea cable connectivity, financial-services demand, and ecosystem density.
- Most large operators need Mumbai presence for credibility with hyperscalers and BFSI customers.

### Chennai
- Fastest-rising secondary cluster due to cables, land availability, and large campus economics.
- Particularly relevant for new hyperscale capacity and south-India resilience strategies.

### Hyderabad and Noida
- Both are strategic expansion cities rather than niche nodes.
- Hyderabad benefits from cloud/IT ecosystem demand; Noida benefits from north-India enterprise demand and campus-scale development.

## Patterns and Gaps
- **Concentration**: the market is concentrated, but not winner-take-all. Large customers still want regional redundancy.
- **Operator differentiation**:
  - `STT GDC India`: breadth and hyperscale credibility
  - `CtrlS`: domestic enterprise/government trust and large domestic capacity
  - `Yotta`: flagship hyperscale campus strategy
  - `Sify`: enterprise networking plus colocation stack
  - `Nxtra`: telecom-distribution advantage
- **Gap in public data**: operator websites rarely give standardized live MW, utilization, and under-construction splits, so cross-operator comparisons remain partly directional.

## Recommendations
1. For investor or partner mapping, use a two-step lens: `market-level capacity from broker reports` plus `operator-level proof points from company disclosures`.
2. For customer strategy, shortlist `STT GDC India`, `CtrlS`, `Yotta`, `Sify`, and `Nxtra` first; then segment by use case rather than trying to force a single national ranking.
3. Treat Mumbai and Chennai as mandatory reference clusters. Any operator without strong proof in those markets should be treated as second-tier for hyperscale strategy.
