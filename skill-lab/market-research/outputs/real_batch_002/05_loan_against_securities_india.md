# Loan Against Securities Market in India

## Executive Summary
The exact `total AUM outstanding` for the Indian Loan Against Securities market is **not defensible from public evidence alone**. That is the central conclusion and the main correction versus Batch 001. What *is* defensible is a proxy view built from RBI system categories and disclosed lender books: scheduled commercial banks had roughly **Rs 9,800 crore** of loans against shares and bonds outstanding in mid-2025, while RBI data cited in market reporting put NBFC lending against shares and bonds at about **Rs 22,432 crore** as of December 2024. Combined, those proxies suggest a visible system bucket of roughly **Rs 32,000 crore**, but that is **not the same thing** as a clean LAS-only market AUM number.

## Definitions
- **Market**: secured lending to individuals or businesses against financial securities such as listed shares, mutual funds, bonds, and similar investment assets.
- **Geography**: India.
- **Time horizon**: current proxy read plus a 5-year outlook.
- **Primary requested metric**: total AUM outstanding.

## Primary Metric Call
- **Requested metric**: total AUM outstanding in the Indian LAS market.
- **Defensible estimate**: **insufficient evidence for a defensible exact LAS-only estimate**.
- **Why not**: public system data is usually reported in broader categories such as `loans against shares and bonds` rather than a clean standalone LAS series; lender disclosures also mix LAS with capital-market finance, margin funding, or other secured products.
- **Best available proxy metrics**:
  1. **Scheduled commercial banks**: about **Rs 9,800 crore** in loans against shares and bonds outstanding in mid-2025. Source: RBI-linked reporting in [Economic Times](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/markets/stocks/news/rbi-proposes-higher-lending-limits-against-shares-ipos/articleshow/124253475.cms) and [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/markets/news/rbi-to-raise-ipo-financing-and-loans-against-shares-limits-125100101020_1.html).
  2. **NBFCs**: about **Rs 22,432 crore** in lending against shares and bonds as of December 2024, cited from RBI bulletin coverage in [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/markets/news/rbi-to-raise-ipo-financing-and-loans-against-shares-limits-125100101020_1.html).
  3. **Disclosed lender books**: some public lenders expose sub-books, but they often combine LAS with margin or capital-market finance, so they are useful as directional evidence rather than clean market sizing.
- **Proxy total**: roughly **Rs 32,000 crore** for the broader `shares and bonds` secured-lending bucket.
- **Important caution**: this proxy may **overstate** or **misclassify** pure LAS because the category includes products adjacent to LAS.

## Market Sizing Structure
### TAM (broad opportunity lens)
- A broad secured-lending opportunity exists because India continues to financialize household savings, equity participation keeps rising, and lenders are widening product access against listed assets.
- However, public data quality is not good enough to claim a precise TAM without making heavy assumptions.

### SAM (serviceable market for formal lenders)
- The formal serviceable market is concentrated among banks, NBFCs, brokers/wealth-linked lenders, and digitally enabled secured-credit products targeting affluent retail and SME borrowers.
- The closest defensible serviceable proxy remains the combined system bucket of about **Rs 32,000 crore** in loans against shares and bonds.

### SOM (obtainable market)
- Any lender-specific SOM depends more on target segment, branch/distribution access, and risk appetite than on abstract national market size.
- For a new or growth-stage entrant, realistic SOM should be framed as share of high-quality affluent borrowers or wealth-linked SME borrowers, not share of the entire proxy bucket.

## Growth Outlook: Next 5 Years
### Key growth drivers
- **Financialization of savings**: more demat accounts, rising MF/equity participation, and a larger pool of pledge-able assets improve addressability.
- **Regulatory easing**: RBI has proposed materially higher limits for loans against shares and IPO financing, which could expand ticket sizes for regulated lenders. Source: [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/markets/news/rbi-to-raise-ipo-financing-and-loans-against-shares-limits-125100101020_1.html).
- **Digital distribution**: brokers, wealth platforms, and digital lenders can make LAS more accessible and operationally faster.
- **Working-capital use cases**: affluent individuals and market-linked SMEs increasingly use securities-backed credit as a liquidity bridge rather than selling assets.

### Headwinds
- **Market volatility**: falling collateral values can sharply affect utilization, demand, and lender risk appetite.
- **Product-definition ambiguity**: lender disclosures often blur LAS with margin funding and broader capital-market finance.
- **Regulatory sensitivity**: because the product touches capital markets, leverage, and retail investor protection, growth can be capped by prudential tightening.

### 5-year directional view
- The market should grow faster than traditional retail secured lending niches because the underlying wealth base is expanding and operational friction is falling.
- But public data quality is still too weak to support a precise CAGR claim without relying on private paid reports or aggressive assumptions.

## Evidence-Backed Landscape Snapshot
| Lens | Evidence | What it tells us |

## So What
- For investor briefing, the honest answer is that **exact LAS-only AUM is not publicly defensible** today.
- For strategy, the best working view is a `broader shares-and-bonds secured-lending proxy market` of about **Rs 32,000 crore**, with upside from financialization and regulatory easing.
- For diligence, the next step is not a prettier market-size slide; it is obtaining lender-level segment disclosures or paid datasets that separate LAS from adjacent capital-market credit products.

## Recommendations
1. Use **Rs 32,000 crore** only as a proxy bucket and label it exactly as `banks + NBFC loans against shares and bonds`, not as pure LAS market AUM.
2. If this topic is going into an investor deck, put the insufficiency note on the page, not in the appendix.
3. For a real go-to-market decision, build the market from the bottom up using target customer wealth bands, eligible collateral pools, and lender penetration rather than relying on weak top-down public series.
