# Loan Against Securities Market in India

## Executive Summary
The exact `total AUM outstanding` for the Indian Loan Against Securities market is still **not publicly defensible as a clean single number**. The correct answer remains an insufficiency call on the exact metric. But the earlier report was still too shallow because it did not show a proper source ledger, denominator logic, or scenario sensitivity.

The best current public frame is:
- **Observed disclosed proxy bucket**: roughly **Rs 32,000 crore** from the combined `bank + NBFC loans against shares and bonds` category.
- **Interpretation**: this is a useful upper-bound proxy for the formal visible LAS-adjacent market, not a pure LAS number.
- **Working scenario range for pure LAS within that bucket**: approximately **Rs 18,000-28,000 crore**, depending on how much of the reported category is true retail / SME LAS versus other share-backed or capital-markets-adjacent exposures.

That range is a scenario construct, not an official market figure.

## Definitions
- **Market in scope**: secured credit against listed equity shares, mutual funds, bonds, and similar financial securities for retail, affluent, and SME borrowers.
- **Geography**: India.
- **Time horizon**: current market read plus a 5-year outlook.
- **Primary requested metric**: total AUM outstanding.

## Primary Metric Call
- **Requested metric**: total AUM outstanding in the Indian LAS market.
- **Defensible exact estimate**: **insufficient evidence for a defensible exact LAS-only number from public sources alone**.
- **Why**:
  1. Public system categories are usually reported as `loans against shares and bonds`, not pure LAS.
  2. Lender disclosures often mix LAS with promoter funding, capital-market finance, or other secured-against-securities books.
  3. Public sources do not provide a standardized, lender-by-lender LAS-only break-out.

## What Is Defensible Today
### 1. Observed proxy bucket
- **Banks**: about **Rs 9,800 crore** in loans against shares and bonds outstanding in mid-2025.
- **NBFCs**: about **Rs 22,432 crore** in loans against shares and bonds as of December 2024.
- **Combined visible bucket**: roughly **Rs 32,000 crore**.

### 2. Why the proxy is useful but imperfect
- It captures the formal system’s disclosed exposure to securities-backed lending.
- It likely includes some exposures that are broader than classic retail / SME LAS.
- It may still miss lender-specific structures that are not cleanly categorized in public data.

## Scenario Range for Pure LAS
### Assumptions & Sensitivity
| Assumption | Conservative case | Base case | Aggressive case | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Share of the disclosed `loans against shares and bonds` bucket that is true LAS rather than adjacent share-backed credit | 55% | 70% | 85% | This is the single biggest source of uncertainty |
| Formal-lender visibility captures most of the economically relevant LAS market | High | Medium | Medium/Low | Hidden or differently classified exposures change the inferred range |
| Retail / affluent borrower usage grows with financialization | Moderate | Strong | Strong | Affects 5-year outlook more than current AUM |

### Implied range
- **Conservative**: `Rs 17,700 crore` (`55%` of roughly `Rs 32,200 crore`)
- **Base**: `Rs 22,500 crore` (`70%` of roughly `Rs 32,200 crore`)
- **Aggressive**: `Rs 27,400 crore` (`85%` of roughly `Rs 32,200 crore`)

### Sensitivity takeaway
The market-size answer is dominated by one uncertainty: how much of the public `shares and bonds` lending bucket is true LAS versus broader securities-backed credit. That is why any point estimate stated without this caveat is misleading.

## Market Structure
| Segment | Role in the market | What public evidence suggests |
|---|---|---|
| Banks | Formal secured-credit providers, especially for affluent borrowers | Visible but smaller than NBFC proxy bucket |
| NBFCs | Larger disclosed proxy bucket and often more flexible product structuring | Likely the dominant formal segment by public proxy |
| Brokers / wealth-linked lenders | Distribution and customer-acquisition edge | Important commercially, but public book disclosure is inconsistent |
| Digital lending / fintech overlays | Improve process speed and onboarding | Growing relevance, but still depend on regulated balance-sheet capacity |

## Growth Outlook: Next 5 Years
### Drivers
- **Financialization of savings**: CDSL data shows a very large and still expanding investor-account base, which increases the pool of potentially pledge-able assets.
- **Mutual fund and capital-market depth**: AMFI data continues to show a very large and expanding financial-asset base that can support secured-liquidity products.
- **Regulatory easing on limits**: RBI’s move to raise certain lending limits against shares and IPO-related financing points to a more expandable operating envelope for regulated lenders.
- **Digital distribution**: faster pledge, onboarding, and risk workflows make LAS more usable as a liquidity product rather than a niche branch-led offering.

### Headwinds
- **Market volatility**: the product is structurally pro-cyclical because collateral value can compress quickly.
- **Regulatory caution**: any retail-investor protection concern can slow or cap growth.
- **Disclosure ambiguity**: weak category transparency makes external market tracking noisier than the product’s real growth.

### 5-year view
A reasonable directional expectation is that LAS grows faster than many traditional secured retail-credit niches because the underlying collateral base is broadening and the product is operationally easier to deliver. But a precise public CAGR still requires either paid datasets or lender-level disclosures not consistently available in public materials.

## So What
- For an investor deck, the disciplined answer is **not** “the LAS market is Rs X crore.” It is: `exact LAS AUM is not publicly defensible; the best visible formal proxy bucket is roughly Rs 32,000 crore, with a working pure-LAS scenario range of about Rs 18,000-28,000 crore.`
- For strategy, the product looks attractive because the collateral base is expanding and digital delivery is improving.
- For diligence, the next real unlock is lender-level segmentation, not more secondary-market commentary.

## Recommendations
1. Use the `Rs 32,000 crore` figure only as a `shares-and-bonds secured-lending proxy`, never as a pure LAS number.
2. If you need a market-size slide, show a **range with assumptions** rather than a single-point number.
3. For a go-to-market decision, build bottom-up scenarios using target borrower wealth bands, eligible collateral pools, and lender penetration assumptions instead of relying on weak public category data.
